# GENERAL SAMPLE 1
## Innovation / R&D Template

> **HOW TO USE THIS SAMPLE**
>
> This is a general-purpose template for an unsolicited proposal centered on a **novel technology or research-driven innovation**. Use it when your idea involves a new invention, a proof of concept, a patent, a proprietary process, or an applied research effort that you would like a federal research-oriented agency to fund, pilot, or adopt.
>
> The sample below is a fully-written fictional proposal. Company, people, technology, and numbers are all made up. The **structure, regulation citations, and FAR 15.609 markings are real and compliant**. To adapt this template to your own situation, replace the company name, key personnel, technical approach, and dollar figures. Keep the structural order, the required legends, and the mission-alignment pattern intact.
>
> A "HOW TO ADAPT THIS TEMPLATE" section appears at the end of this document with a checklist of everything you should change.

## PART 2 — TECHNICAL INFORMATION (FAR 15.605(b))

**2.1 Title and Abstract**

**Title:** Graphene-Composite Reverse Osmosis Membranes for Extended Shipboard Potable Water Generation

**Abstract (197 words):**

Nova Materials, Inc. proposes an 18-month research and development effort to qualify a next-generation graphene-composite reverse osmosis (RO) membrane for shipboard potable water generation on U.S. Navy vessels. Current shipboard RO membranes require replacement every 3–5 years due to biofouling and performance degradation, consuming significant logistics resources and creating maintenance downtime during deployments. Nova Materials has developed a proprietary graphene-oxide functionalization process that produces RO membranes with 2.3 times the operational lifespan and 18% lower energy consumption than current Navy-standard membranes, based on 14 months of laboratory testing. This proposal seeks ONR support to (1) complete accelerated life-cycle testing of the membrane against Navy-standard operating profiles, (2) produce a pilot quantity of full-size membranes for shipboard evaluation, and (3) generate the qualification data package necessary for formal Navy adoption consideration. Total estimated effort: $1,847,500 over 18 months. Nova Materials holds U.S. Patent Pending 18/XXX,XXX on the underlying functionalization process and is the sole entity with the capability to produce membranes with the performance characteristics demonstrated in internal testing. This work directly supports ONR Code 33's mission area in shipboard systems and crew sustainment.

**2.2 Objectives**

Nova Materials proposes the following specific, measurable objectives for this effort:

1. **Complete accelerated life-cycle testing** of the graphene-composite RO membrane against Navy-standard operating profiles, demonstrating ≥ 2.0x operational lifespan improvement and ≥ 15% energy reduction compared to incumbent membranes, measured against MIL-STD-2036 and applicable NAVSEA standards.

2. **Produce a pilot quantity** of 40 full-size (8-inch diameter × 40-inch length) membranes meeting Navy physical and performance specifications, suitable for at-sea evaluation aboard a single qualifying vessel class.

3. **Deliver a complete qualification data package** including test protocols, raw test data, failure-mode analysis, maintenance requirements, and cost-of-ownership modeling, formatted to support a Navy formal adoption decision.

4. **Develop a transition path** to support Navy formal adoption, including identification of qualified production facilities, supply chain risk assessment, and a preliminary cost model for full-fleet deployment.

**2.3 Method of Approach**

The proposed effort will execute in four phases over 18 months.

**Phase 1 — Design Refinement and Test Planning (Months 1–3).** Nova Materials will finalize the membrane design against Navy requirements documents provided under a non-disclosure agreement. Our team will collaborate with Navy subject matter experts at NAVSEA to ensure the membrane specifications match operational profiles for the target vessel class. Deliverables include a Test Plan, a Design Requirements Document, and a Hazard Analysis.

**Phase 2 — Accelerated Life-Cycle Testing (Months 4–10).** The membranes will undergo accelerated testing in Nova Materials' existing test facility, which houses two MIL-STD-compliant flow loops. Testing will simulate 10 years of shipboard operation compressed into 7 months. Biofouling exposure, salinity variation, temperature cycling, and pressure variation will all be applied. Deliverables include Monthly Test Reports, a Mid-Phase Data Review with ONR, and an End-of-Phase Test Report.

**Phase 3 — Pilot Production (Months 9–14, overlapping Phase 2).** In parallel with the final months of testing, Nova Materials will produce 40 full-size pilot membranes for Navy at-sea evaluation. Production will occur at Nova Materials' existing Raleigh facility, which has the capacity to produce approximately 200 units per year at pilot scale. Deliverables include 40 production-quality membranes packaged to Navy shipping and storage requirements.

**Phase 4 — Qualification Data Package and Transition Planning (Months 15–18).** Nova Materials will consolidate all test data, production records, and supplier information into a single Qualification Data Package formatted to the Navy's preferred review structure. Nova Materials will also conduct a transition workshop with ONR and NAVSEA to brief the results and identify the most likely path to formal adoption. Deliverables include the Qualification Data Package and a Final Technical Report.

**2.4 Anticipated Results**

At the conclusion of this 18-month effort, the Navy will have:

- A fully-tested, mission-ready alternative RO membrane demonstrating (per Nova Materials' internal testing expectations) more than double the service lifespan and ~15–20% lower energy consumption than incumbent membranes.
- A complete qualification data package suitable for submission to the Navy's formal adoption review process.
- Forty full-size pilot membranes available for at-sea installation and evaluation on a qualifying vessel class.
- A clear transition path for full-fleet deployment, including supply chain and production capacity analysis.

**Potential value to the Navy over a 10-year fleet deployment** (based on Nova Materials' preliminary modeling): approximately $60–80 million in reduced membrane replacement costs across the fleet, plus reduced fuel consumption from improved energy efficiency, plus reduced maintenance downtime during deployments. Nova Materials acknowledges these are estimates based on internal modeling and would be refined during the qualification effort.

**2.5 Mission Support Statement**

This effort directly supports the Office of Naval Research's mission to *"plan, foster, and encourage scientific research in recognition of its paramount importance as related to the maintenance of future naval power, and the preservation of national security,"* as specified in 10 U.S.C. § 8911. Specifically, the proposed work falls within ONR Code 33 (Sea Warfare and Weapons), which sponsors research into shipboard systems that improve crew sustainment, operational endurance, and at-sea logistics independence.

The ONR Naval Science and Technology Strategy identifies **crew health and sustainment** and **reduced logistics footprint** as priority investment areas. The graphene-composite RO membrane directly addresses both priorities: by extending membrane life and reducing energy consumption, the technology reduces the logistics burden on the fleet (fewer replacement membranes to ship and store) and improves operational endurance (less unplanned maintenance downtime during deployments).

Nova Materials believes this effort represents an opportunity for ONR to take a validated laboratory-scale innovation across the technology readiness "valley of death" into an operational Navy capability, consistent with ONR's mandate to transition research into fleet capability.
