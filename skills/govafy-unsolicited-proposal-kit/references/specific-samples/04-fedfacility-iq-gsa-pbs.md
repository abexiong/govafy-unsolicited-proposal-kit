A weaker proposal would duplicate GSALink. This sample is positioned as a **retrofit overlay for aging buildings that do not have modern building automation systems** — which is a different and narrower problem than what GSALink addresses. This is a good teaching example for members who want to propose facility services to GSA PBS.

## PART 2 — TECHNICAL INFORMATION (FAR 15.605(b))

**2.1 Title and Abstract**

**Title:** FedFacility IQ — Wireless-Sensor Retrofit Platform for Energy Optimization of Aging GSA-Owned Federal Buildings

**Abstract (198 words):**

Civic Energy Insights, LLC proposes FedFacility IQ, a wireless-sensor retrofit platform designed to deliver 20–30% HVAC and lighting energy reduction in GSA-owned federal buildings that currently lack modern building automation systems. Unlike GSA's existing GSALink fault detection and diagnostics application — which requires an underlying BAS for data input — FedFacility IQ installs a low-cost wireless sensor network (temperature, humidity, CO₂, occupancy, lighting level, plug load) over the existing mechanical and electrical infrastructure, streams the data to a cloud analytics engine, and continuously optimizes setpoints and runtime schedules using machine learning. The platform is designed specifically for the segment of the GSA portfolio where installing a full BAS is cost-prohibitive or operationally disruptive. In a three-year deployment at six commercial and institutional buildings (total 1.4 million square feet) Civic Energy achieved measured energy savings averaging 26.3% with a 14-month simple payback. This proposal offers a 24-month pilot deployment at four GSA-owned buildings selected by PBS, with measurement-and-verification (M&V) per FEMP protocols and a deployment playbook for broader rollout. Total estimated price: $2,910,000. FedFacility IQ complements — and does not replace — GSALink.

**2.2 Objectives**

1. **Deploy FedFacility IQ at four GSA-owned buildings** (total 800,000–1,200,000 square feet) selected by PBS to represent the portfolio of aging federal buildings without modern BAS, within nine months of contract award.

2. **Achieve average HVAC and lighting energy reduction ≥ 20%** across the four pilot buildings relative to a baseline established under FEMP M&V protocols.

3. **Maintain or improve occupant comfort metrics** (thermal comfort, CO₂ levels, lighting adequacy) at the pilot buildings during the deployment.

4. **Deliver a GSA-specific deployment playbook and cost model** supporting enterprise-wide rollout across the GSA-owned portfolio.

**2.3 Method of Approach**

**Phase 1 — Site Selection, Survey, and Baseline (Months 1–6).** Civic Energy will work with PBS to select four pilot buildings, complete on-site energy surveys, install reference metering, and establish 90-day FEMP-compliant baselines. Deliverables include a Baseline Energy Report for each building.

**Phase 2 — Sensor Installation and Platform Deployment (Months 7–12).** Wireless sensors and edge nodes will be installed at each pilot building. Installation does not require shutdown of existing building systems and can be completed during normal operating hours. FedFacility IQ will begin streaming data, initial optimization will occur, and the joint working group will review the first operational month. Deliverables include an Installation Report per building and a First-Month Operations Review.

**Phase 3 — Active Optimization and Monthly Measurement (Months 13–22).** Ten months of active optimization with monthly M&V reporting, quarterly deep-dive technical reviews, and a Mid-Phase Review at month 18. Civic Energy field engineers will visit each pilot building at least quarterly.

**Phase 4 — Final M&V, Playbook, and Transition (Months 23–24).** Civic Energy will consolidate all M&V data into a final Energy Performance Report, develop the GSA-specific Deployment Playbook, and brief PBS leadership on results and enterprise rollout options.

**2.4 Anticipated Results**

- **Four operational deployments** with at least 10 months of continuous optimization data and FEMP-compliant M&V reports.
- **Measured annual energy savings** across the pilot buildings totaling an estimated $420,000–$650,000 per year (based on pilot building sizes and current GSA energy rates).
- **Preserved or improved occupant comfort metrics** validated against ASHRAE standards.
- **A deployment playbook and enterprise cost model** supporting PBS consideration of broader rollout.
- **Compatibility with existing GSA systems** including GSALink, the GSA Smart Buildings program, and the Building Technology and Security Division's integration standards.

**2.5 Mission Support Statement**

This effort directly supports GSA's Public Buildings Service and specifically PBS's Office of Facilities Management. PBS manages a federal building portfolio responsible for significant federal energy consumption and is implementing PBS sustainability, energy efficiency, and net-zero targets as part of its core mission.

GSA's Smart Buildings program — originally focused on advanced metering and fault detection and diagnostics through tools like GSALink — has successfully modernized newer buildings with building automation systems. A substantial portion of the GSA-owned portfolio, however, consists of older buildings where installing a full BAS is cost-prohibitive, operationally disruptive, or constrained by historic-preservation considerations. The PBS Building Technologies Technical Reference Guide acknowledges the challenge of integrating legacy buildings into the smart-building framework.

FedFacility IQ specifically targets this gap: buildings where a full BAS is not practical but where a wireless-sensor overlay can still deliver measurable energy savings. The proposed effort does not duplicate GSALink — the two tools address different parts of the portfolio and can operate in parallel where both apply. Civic Energy has structured this proposal to be explicitly complementary to GSA's existing smart building investments.
