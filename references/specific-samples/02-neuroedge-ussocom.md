# SPECIFIC SAMPLE 2
## NeuroEdge — Wearable Cognitive Performance System for Special Operations Forces
### Submitted to the United States Special Operations Command (USSOCOM)

> **NOTE TO READERS**
>
> This is a fictional sample proposal provided for educational use. The company, people, pilot data, and prices are invented. The agency, regulations, program names (POTFF, Hyper-Enabled Operator/HEO, SOF AT&L), and mission language are real. Use this sample to see how to structure a proof-of-concept R&D proposal in the warfighter performance space. Do not copy and submit it — adapt the structure to your own idea.
>
> **Why this sample works as a teaching example:** USSOCOM publishes Commercial Solutions Openings (CSOs) for innovation. A strong unsolicited proposal in this space has to be clearly different from what could be submitted through the CSO process — otherwise the contracting officer will redirect the submitter to the CSO instead. This sample addresses that concern head-on.

## PART 2 — TECHNICAL INFORMATION (FAR 15.605(b))

**2.1 Title and Abstract**

**Title:** NeuroEdge — Wearable Cognitive Performance Enhancement System for Sustained-Operation SOF Operators

**Abstract (200 words):**

Apex Cognitive Labs, Inc. proposes NeuroEdge, a non-invasive wearable cognitive performance system designed to sustain operator decision-making and reaction time under extended sleep deprivation. NeuroEdge combines a lightweight forehead-worn neurofeedback device, a paired biometric mobile application, and a structured protocol of targeted micro-rest, hydration cues, and cognitive priming. In a 14-week institutional review board–approved pilot with 42 endurance-trained civilian subjects undergoing 72-hour sleep restriction protocols, NeuroEdge users demonstrated an 18.4% improvement in mean simple reaction time and a 23.7% improvement in complex decision-making accuracy compared to a matched control group. The device is non-pharmacological, non-invasive, and requires no implantation or surgical procedure. This proposal seeks USSOCOM SOF AT&L support for a 24-month program consisting of (1) a military-population validation study of 60 SOF-qualified volunteers in partnership with a USSOCOM-designated Human Performance Program (HPP) site, (2) a field-environment refinement of the device for operational durability, and (3) a formal transition assessment for potential USSOCOM adoption as part of the Hyper-Enabled Operator (HEO) program. Total estimated price: $3,640,000. Apex Cognitive Labs holds U.S. Patent 11,XXX,XXX on the underlying neurofeedback approach.

**2.2 Objectives**

1. **Validate NeuroEdge performance in a military-population study** of 60 SOF-qualified volunteers, demonstrating ≥ 15% improvement in mean reaction time and ≥ 18% improvement in complex decision-making accuracy under simulated sustained-operation sleep restriction.

2. **Harden the device and app** for operational durability against SOF field conditions (water, dust, temperature extremes, impact).

3. **Characterize safety and side effects** through IRB-approved human subject monitoring.

4. **Deliver a formal transition assessment report** suitable for supporting a USSOCOM decision on next steps (follow-on prototype buy, CSO transition, Program of Record consideration, or termination).

**2.3 Method of Approach**

**Phase 1 — Study Design, IRB, and Device Preparation (Months 1–5).** Apex Cognitive Labs will finalize a military-population study protocol in coordination with USSOCOM's Preservation of the Force and Family (POTFF) program office and will submit the protocol to an appropriate IRB (either USSOCOM-affiliated or an independent commercial IRB acceptable to USSOCOM). Fifty devices will be configured for study use. Deliverables include IRB-approved Study Protocol, Device Preparation Report, and Participant Consent Package.

**Phase 2 — Military-Population Validation Study (Months 6–15).** Sixty SOF-qualified volunteers will complete the protocol at one or more USSOCOM-designated HPP sites (USASOC, NSW, MARSOC, or AFSOC). Protocol involves baseline assessment, randomization into experimental and control groups, a 72-hour sleep restriction exposure with standardized cognitive task batteries, a recovery window, and follow-up at 14 and 30 days. Deliverables include Monthly Study Reports, a Mid-Study Data Review, and a draft Study Results Report.

**Phase 3 — Operational Durability Refinement (Months 10–20, overlapping Phase 2).** In parallel with the validation study, Apex engineering staff will complete environmental testing of the device against MIL-STD-810 (relevant provisions) for water, dust, shock, vibration, and temperature. Device modifications identified during the study will be incorporated. Deliverables include an Environmental Test Report and a Final Device Reference Design.

**Phase 4 — Transition Assessment and Reporting (Months 21–24).** Apex will consolidate study results, durability testing, safety monitoring, and cost-of-ownership modeling into a formal Transition Assessment Report and will brief USSOCOM leadership. Deliverables include the Transition Assessment Report and a Final Technical Report.

**2.4 Anticipated Results**

- **A validated, military-population data set** demonstrating (or failing to demonstrate) NeuroEdge's effectiveness under operational sleep restriction.
- **A field-hardened, operationally durable version of the device**, ready for potential follow-on acquisition.
- **A safety and side-effect characterization**, required for any future operational deployment.
- **A transition-ready assessment** supporting USSOCOM's decision on whether to pursue the technology through a follow-on CSO award, Program of Record, or other acquisition pathway.

**2.5 Mission Support Statement**

This effort directly supports USSOCOM's mission to *"provide the Nation with an agile, adaptable, and capable SOF force to address irregular, asymmetric, and unconventional threats."* Within that mission, USSOCOM has prioritized operator performance optimization through the Preservation of the Force and Family (POTFF) program and the Hyper-Enabled Operator (HEO) initiative under SOF AT&L.

The HEO initiative, as publicly described by USSOCOM leadership, seeks to enhance the SOF operator's cognition by increasing situational awareness, reducing cognitive load, and accelerating decision-making at the tactical edge. NeuroEdge directly addresses the *cognition* element of HEO — specifically the degradation of decision-making and reaction time under sustained operations with restricted sleep, a well-documented challenge for SOF operators in prolonged missions.

Apex Cognitive Labs understands that USSOCOM SOF AT&L maintains an active Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) process for rapid innovation acquisition. Apex has reviewed the current CSO topics and confirmed that no active CSO topic addresses non-pharmacological wearable cognitive enhancement for sleep-restricted operators. This unsolicited proposal is therefore not an attempt to circumvent a competitive acquisition process. If USSOCOM determines that the topic is better suited to the CSO pathway, Apex is prepared to respond to a corresponding CSO instead.
