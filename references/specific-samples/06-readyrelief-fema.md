A weaker proposal would try to replicate these capabilities. This sample is positioned as a **predictive AI overlay** that sits on top of FEMA's existing logistics infrastructure — using 72–96-hour pre-disaster forecasting to reposition supplies before the incident — which is distinct from what LogVIZ does today.

## PART 2 — TECHNICAL INFORMATION (FAR 15.605(b))

**2.1 Title and Abstract**

**Title:** ReadyRelief — Predictive AI Overlay for FEMA Pre-Disaster Logistics Repositioning

**Abstract (199 words):**

Predictive Resilience, LLC proposes ReadyRelief, a predictive AI platform designed as a decision-support overlay on FEMA's existing logistics infrastructure — not a replacement. ReadyRelief ingests historical disaster response data, real-time weather forecasts, demographic and social-vulnerability indices, road and port status data, and local retail commodity availability signals, and produces 72-to-96-hour forecasts of where and how much of each Initial Response Resource (IRR) commodity FEMA will likely need before a declared disaster. The platform is designed to integrate with FEMA's Logistics Supply Chain Management System and to complement FEMA's existing LogVIZ/SAVER2 situational awareness viewer by providing a forward-looking prediction layer where current tools focus on current-state visibility. In a 24-month retrospective analysis using historical data from 18 major disaster declarations, the ReadyRelief model correctly forecast 72-hour IRR demand within 15% of actual for 14 of 18 events. This proposal offers FEMA a 24-month pilot deployment supporting two FEMA Regions and three of the eight National Logistics Staging Areas, with retrospective validation, live-forecast operational use during two hurricane seasons, and a formal transition report. Total estimated price: $3,950,000. Predictive Resilience is a HUBZone and SDVOSB firm.

**2.2 Objectives**

1. **Deploy ReadyRelief in a decision-support posture** at two FEMA Regions (selected by FEMA LMD) and three of the eight FEMA Distribution Centers (Atlanta, Fort Worth, Cumberland, Frederick, Moffett Field, Puerto Rico, Hawaii, or Guam — selection by FEMA).

2. **Demonstrate 72-hour demand forecasts** within 15% of actual for the six Initial Response Resources (water, tarps, meals, cots, blue roof sheeting, blankets) across at least 75% of events in the pilot period.

3. **Validate repositioning recommendations** against actual FEMA repositioning decisions made during the pilot, documenting where ReadyRelief recommendations matched, exceeded, or fell short of human-expert decisions.

4. **Deliver a formal transition assessment** supporting FEMA LMD's decision on whether to adopt ReadyRelief as a recommended decision-support tool for broader use across all eight Distribution Centers.

**2.3 Method of Approach**

**Phase 1 — Baseline, Data Integration, and Retrospective Validation (Months 1–7).** Predictive Resilience will work with FEMA LMD and LogVIZ/SAVER2 system owners to establish data integration (one-way read-only connectors — ReadyRelief does not write into existing FEMA systems). Historical data from at least 20 disasters over the past 5 years will be used for retrospective model validation at the two selected pilot Regions. Deliverables include a Data Integration Document, a Retrospective Validation Report, and the FEMA-specific model configuration.

**Phase 2 — Live Decision-Support Deployment — Season 1 (Months 8–17).** During the first full hurricane season of the pilot, ReadyRelief will be operated in parallel with FEMA's existing decision processes at the two pilot Regions. ReadyRelief forecasts and repositioning recommendations will be delivered to FEMA Regional Response Coordinators and LMD staff as a decision-support input — FEMA retains all authority over actual logistics decisions. Every ReadyRelief recommendation will be logged and compared against actual FEMA decisions and actual outcomes. Deliverables include Weekly Forecast Logs during active events and an End-of-Season 1 Analysis Report.

**Phase 3 — Live Decision-Support Deployment — Season 2 (Months 18–22).** Second hurricane season, same structure as Phase 2, incorporating any model refinements identified in Season 1.

**Phase 4 — Transition Assessment and Reporting (Months 23–24).** Predictive Resilience will consolidate all retrospective and live-deployment data into a Transition Assessment Report, including a cost-benefit analysis of broader deployment and recommendations for FEMA's decision.

**2.4 Anticipated Results**

- **Two complete hurricane seasons** of live decision-support data showing ReadyRelief performance under operational conditions.
- **A retrospective validation dataset** covering at least 20 historical disasters showing model performance against ground truth.
- **Quantified value** — the retrospective analysis and live deployment will be used to estimate whether earlier repositioning decisions would have reduced time-to-distribution for affected populations and reduced emergency sourcing costs.
- **A transition-ready assessment** supporting FEMA LMD's decision on broader adoption.

Based on preliminary modeling against historical events, Predictive Resilience estimates that effective 72–96-hour forecasts could enable pre-positioning decisions that reduce time-to-first-commodity-delivery by 18–28 hours in affected areas and reduce emergency sourcing costs by displacing some emergency local procurement with pre-positioned stock. These estimates are preliminary and would be refined during the pilot.

**2.5 Mission Support Statement**

This effort directly supports FEMA's Logistics Management Directorate (LMD), specifically the Distribution Management Division. LMD serves as the National Logistics Coordinator under Emergency Support Function #7 and is responsible for pre-positioning and distributing initial response resources during domestic disasters. LMD maintains Initial Response Resources at eight Distribution Centers — Atlanta, Fort Worth, Cumberland, Frederick, Moffett Field, Puerto Rico, Hawaii, and Guam — and operates the LogVIZ/SAVER2 situational awareness system to track commodities, vendor availability, and distribution operations.

FEMA's current decision processes rely heavily on LogVIZ/SAVER2 for situational awareness of current state, supplemented by human expertise and historical judgment for forward-looking decisions about pre-disaster repositioning. The distinction matters: LogVIZ answers "where are our resources now?" very well; it is not a predictive tool.

ReadyRelief is specifically designed to fill this gap as a decision-support overlay, not as a replacement for LogVIZ or SAVER2. ReadyRelief produces forward-looking forecasts that complement LogVIZ's current-state visibility. All repositioning decisions remain with FEMA leadership; ReadyRelief only provides recommendations for human review.

This proposal does not seek access to write data into FEMA systems, does not seek to modify existing FEMA processes, and does not seek to replace any existing FEMA contract or capability. It offers a purely additive decision-support capability that has been structured specifically to be compatible with FEMA's existing infrastructure and authority model.
