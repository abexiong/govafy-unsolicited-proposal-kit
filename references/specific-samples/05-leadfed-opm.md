# SPECIFIC SAMPLE 5
## LeadFed — SES Candidate Development and Executive Core Qualification Platform
### Submitted to the Office of Personnel Management (OPM)

> **NOTE TO READERS**
>
> This is a fictional sample proposal provided for educational use. The company, people, pilot data, and prices are invented. The agency, the SES Candidate Development Program (SESCDP) framework, the Executive Core Qualifications (ECQs), and OPM's policy role are real.
>
> **Important current context:** The Federal Executive Institute (FEI), OPM's long-standing executive development center, was dismantled in February 2025. Individual agencies continue to run OPM-approved SES Candidate Development Programs (SESCDPs) and still need training and coaching components to meet the OPM standards. In December 2025, OPM published *Ensuring Consistent and Rigorous Standards for Senior Executive Service Candidate Development Programs*, signaling tightened standards. This sample is structured to address OPM's current oversight role rather than a specific agency's SESCDP.
>
> **Why this sample works as a teaching example:** Services proposals — especially training and HR services — are often weakened by vague outcomes and undifferentiated approaches. This sample is structured to demonstrate measurable outcomes, a specific proprietary methodology, and a clear alignment to OPM's current policy direction.

## PART 2 — TECHNICAL INFORMATION (FAR 15.605(b))

**2.1 Title and Abstract**

**Title:** LeadFed — SES Candidate Development Platform Aligned to OPM Executive Core Qualification Standards

**Abstract (197 words):**

Ascend Federal Leadership, Inc. proposes LeadFed, a blended online and one-on-one coaching platform designed to support individual federal agencies in meeting OPM's requirements for Senior Executive Service Candidate Development Programs (SESCDPs). LeadFed combines (1) a proprietary ECQ Readiness Assessment that measures candidates against the five Executive Core Qualifications, (2) a structured 120-hour online curriculum aligned to the SESCDP training requirements for strategic planning, financial management, human capital management, government efficiency, and accountability, (3) one-on-one executive coaching by certified ECQ coaches with direct SES experience, and (4) a qualification package development workshop that has historically improved QRB pass rates in pilot deployments. In a 24-month pilot with three individual agencies covering 62 GS-14 and GS-15 candidates, LeadFed participants completed their SESCDP requirements at a 94% rate (versus a 71% baseline), and QRB first-pass certification rates improved from 63% to 87%. This proposal offers OPM a 24-month pilot cooperative deployment across three OPM-designated agencies plus a final standards alignment report supporting OPM's December 2025 SESCDP policy update. Total estimated price: $2,280,000. Ascend Federal Leadership is an SBA-certified 8(a) and Woman-Owned Small Business.

**2.2 Objectives**

1. **Deploy LeadFed with three OPM-designated federal agencies' SESCDPs**, covering approximately 75–100 GS-14/15 candidates in total.

2. **Achieve SESCDP completion rate ≥ 90%** for LeadFed-supported candidates, compared to a historical baseline established from prior cohorts at each participating agency.

3. **Achieve first-pass QRB certification rate ≥ 85%** for LeadFed-supported candidates who submit qualification packages during the pilot period.

4. **Deliver a formal alignment report** documenting how LeadFed supports the SESCDP requirements in OPM's December 2025 policy guidance and producing a recommended-practices playbook for broader OPM use.

**2.3 Method of Approach**

**Phase 1 — Agency Selection and Configuration (Months 1–5).** Ascend will work with OPM to identify three partner agencies willing to pilot LeadFed as part of their existing SESCDPs. Ascend will configure the platform for each agency's existing SESCDP structure, conduct kickoff workshops with each agency's SESCDP managers, and identify the candidate cohort at each site. Deliverables include Partner Agreements with each agency, a Configuration Document, and Candidate Cohort Rosters.

**Phase 2 — Cohort Launch and Assessment (Months 6–9).** Each candidate completes the ECQ Readiness Assessment, meets with a certified coach to review results, and develops an individualized development plan (aligned to OPM's Executive Development Plan template). Cohorts begin the online curriculum. Deliverables include Assessment Reports, Individualized Development Plans, and Cohort Launch Reports.

**Phase 3 — Coaching and Curriculum Delivery (Months 10–20).** Structured 100-plus-hour curriculum, monthly coaching sessions, peer-learning cohort events, and a qualification package workshop in month 18. Deliverables include Monthly Progress Reports, Mid-Pilot Review at month 14, and Quarterly Coaching Summaries.

**Phase 4 — Outcomes Reporting and Standards Alignment (Months 21–24).** Ascend will consolidate outcomes data (completion rates, QRB results, participant feedback, manager feedback) into a Final Outcomes Report. Ascend will also produce a Standards Alignment Report documenting exactly how LeadFed addresses each requirement in OPM's December 2025 SESCDP policy guidance. Deliverables include the Final Outcomes Report, the Standards Alignment Report, and a briefing to OPM SES Policy leadership.

**2.4 Anticipated Results**

- **75–100 GS-14/15 candidates supported** across three agencies over 24 months.
- **Measurable improvements** in SESCDP completion and QRB first-pass certification rates versus baseline.
- **A documented best-practices playbook** for SESCDP design and operation that OPM can recommend to agencies government-wide.
- **A clear evidence base** showing what a performance-based SESCDP support model looks like, informing OPM's ongoing policy development.

**2.5 Mission Support Statement**

This effort directly supports OPM's statutory responsibility for the Senior Executive Service (5 U.S.C. § 3131 et seq.) and specifically OPM's oversight role for Senior Executive Service Candidate Development Programs under 5 CFR 412.

OPM's December 2025 guidance, *Ensuring Consistent and Rigorous Standards for Senior Executive Service Candidate Development Programs*, directs agencies to tighten their SESCDP standards and to ensure that candidates receive consistent, high-quality preparation for senior executive service. Agencies are required to use the OPM-standardized Executive Development Plan (EDP) template and to ensure candidates complete at least 100 hours of formal interagency or multi-sector training addressing strategic planning, financial management, human capital management, government efficiency, and accountability, plus a developmental assignment of at least 120 continuous days.

LeadFed is designed specifically to help agencies meet these requirements consistently and efficiently without each agency separately building or procuring its own development curriculum. The proposed pilot would give OPM direct visibility into whether an external platform can support agency SESCDP compliance at scale.

This proposal does not seek to replace agency SESCDP management authority or OPM's policy oversight role. Ascend offers only the training, assessment, and coaching components — agencies retain authority over candidate selection, developmental assignments, and recommendation for QRB submission.
