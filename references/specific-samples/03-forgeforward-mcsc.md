# SPECIFIC SAMPLE 3
## ForgeForward — Next-Generation Expeditionary Additive Manufacturing
### Submitted to Marine Corps Systems Command (MCSC)

> **NOTE TO READERS**
>
> This is a fictional sample proposal provided for educational use. The company, people, and prices are invented. The agency, programs (X-FAB, TACFAB, PM CSS), and mission language are real.
>
> **Why this sample works as a critical teaching example:** MCSC **already has** expeditionary additive manufacturing programs — the X-FAB and TACFAB systems managed by Program Manager for Combat Support Systems (PM CSS). A weaker unsolicited proposal pitching "3D printing in a box" to MCSC would fail Test 1 (innovative and unique) and Test 6 (not addressing a previously published requirement) of the FAR 15.603(c) eligibility test on its first read, because MCSC is already doing this. The proposal below illustrates how to position a **genuinely next-generation** innovation in a space the agency has already worked in — focusing on multi-material metal printing, AI-driven part identification from damaged components, drone component production, and contested-environment hardening, none of which are currently part of the baseline X-FAB or TACFAB capability.
>
> This is one of the most important teaching examples in the Govafy.com kit. Members who want to propose to an agency that already has something similar should study this sample carefully.

## PART 2 — TECHNICAL INFORMATION (FAR 15.605(b))

**2.1 Title and Abstract**

**Title:** ForgeForward — Multi-Material Expeditionary Additive Manufacturing Cell with AI-Assisted Reverse Engineering

**Abstract (200 words):**

IronForge Manufacturing, Inc. proposes ForgeForward, a next-generation expeditionary additive manufacturing (AM) cell designed to complement and extend the Marine Corps' existing X-FAB and TACFAB capabilities managed by PM CSS. ForgeForward is distinguished from baseline X-FAB by three specific capabilities not currently part of the MCSC fielded additive capability set: (1) an integrated **multi-material metal printing head** capable of producing end-use-grade parts in titanium, aluminum, and tool steel, extending beyond the polymer-focused X-FAB printer set; (2) an **AI-assisted reverse engineering pipeline** that allows Marines to scan a damaged or worn part and automatically generate a printable CAD model without requiring a pre-existing digital library entry; and (3) a **contested-environment hardening package** including power conditioning, shielded communications, and physical ruggedization designed for operations in environments where cloud connectivity to rear-area design libraries is not available. ForgeForward is designed to fit the same 20-by-20-foot expeditionary shelter footprint as X-FAB, preserving existing transport and logistics concepts. This proposal seeks a 24-month prototype and field user evaluation effort. Total estimated price: $5,820,000. IronForge holds patent-pending intellectual property on the multi-material print head and has no current MCSC contract.

**2.2 Objectives**

1. **Design and build two ForgeForward prototype cells** meeting the physical footprint and transportability parameters of the existing X-FAB shelter system.

2. **Demonstrate multi-material metal printing** of a curated set of 25 representative Marine Corps replacement parts in titanium, aluminum, and tool steel, meeting military specification requirements.

3. **Demonstrate AI-assisted reverse engineering** on a curated set of 15 damaged or worn parts, achieving printable CAD generation within 90 minutes per part without requiring a pre-existing library entry.

4. **Conduct a field user evaluation** with a Marine maintenance unit (ideally at 2nd Maintenance Battalion, Camp Lejeune, the site of the original X-FAB field user evaluation), gathering operational feedback and measurable performance data.

5. **Deliver a comparative capability assessment** against the baseline X-FAB and TACFAB systems, clearly identifying where ForgeForward adds capability and where it does not.

**2.3 Method of Approach**

**Phase 1 — System Design and Integration (Months 1–7).** IronForge will finalize the ForgeForward cell design using the existing 20-by-20-foot expeditionary shelter as a physical baseline, ensuring the cell can be transported on the same equipment and fielded through the same concept of operations as X-FAB. Two prototype cells will be built at IronForge's Norfolk facility. Deliverables include a System Design Document, a Concept of Operations Compatibility Analysis, and delivery of two physical prototype cells.

**Phase 2 — Laboratory Capability Demonstration (Months 8–13).** At IronForge's facility, each cell will demonstrate multi-material metal printing across 25 representative Marine Corps parts selected in coordination with PM CSS. In parallel, the AI reverse engineering pipeline will be demonstrated on 15 damaged part samples. Deliverables include a Parts Catalog Demonstration Report, an AI Pipeline Demonstration Report, and a Mid-Program Review with PM CSS.

**Phase 3 — Field User Evaluation (Months 14–20).** One of the two prototype cells will be transported to a Marine Corps maintenance unit designated by PM CSS for field evaluation. IronForge field service technicians will support initial setup, training, and the first 30 days of operation. The evaluation will be structured similarly to the original X-FAB field user evaluation. Deliverables include a Field User Evaluation Plan, Weekly Evaluation Reports, and a Final Field Evaluation Report.

**Phase 4 — Comparative Assessment and Transition (Months 21–24).** IronForge will consolidate laboratory and field data into a Comparative Capability Assessment against baseline X-FAB and TACFAB, explicitly identifying capability gains and trade-offs. Deliverables include the Comparative Capability Assessment and a Final Technical Report with transition recommendations.

**2.4 Anticipated Results**

- **Two operational prototype cells** available to MCSC for further evaluation or follow-on deployment decisions.
- **A validated multi-material metal printing capability** extending the expeditionary AM capability beyond the current X-FAB polymer focus.
- **A validated AI-assisted reverse engineering pipeline** reducing the dependency on pre-existing digital libraries in forward environments.
- **A field user evaluation dataset** comparable in structure to the original X-FAB evaluation, supporting direct capability comparison.
- **A transition-ready assessment** supporting MCSC's decision on whether to pursue ForgeForward as a capability enhancement to existing programs of record.

**2.5 Mission Support Statement**

This effort directly supports Marine Corps Systems Command and specifically the Program Manager for Combat Support Systems (PM CSS), whose mission includes providing combat support equipment to Marine Corps expeditionary forces. PM CSS's existing X-FAB (Expeditionary Fabrication) and TACFAB (Tactical Fabrication) programs represent the Marine Corps' current baseline for forward-deployed additive manufacturing, enabling Marines to produce essential items and replacement parts at the unit level and reduce dependence on long logistics chains.

ForgeForward is explicitly positioned to **complement and extend** the existing X-FAB and TACFAB programs, not to duplicate them. The three distinguishing capabilities — multi-material metal printing, AI-assisted reverse engineering, and contested-environment hardening — address gaps in the current baseline that are relevant to the evolving Marine Corps operating concept emphasizing distributed operations in contested environments where traditional supply chains may be unreliable or interrupted.

IronForge has reviewed publicly available information on current X-FAB and TACFAB capabilities, SAM.gov records, and published MCSC program office materials. Based on this review, IronForge has identified no current or anticipated competitive solicitation addressing multi-material metal AM capability at the expeditionary level, no active MCSC requirement for AI-assisted reverse engineering in the forward AM context, and no active procurement for contested-environment hardening of the existing X-FAB cell. This informs IronForge's determination that ForgeForward is a proper subject for an unsolicited proposal rather than a response to an existing solicitation.
