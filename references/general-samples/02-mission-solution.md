# GENERAL SAMPLE 2
## Mission-Solution Template

> **HOW TO USE THIS SAMPLE**
>
> This is the most broadly applicable of the three general templates. Use it when you are proposing a **services or operational solution to an agency mission need the agency already has** — but your *approach* is genuinely innovative, even though the problem is recognized. This is the pattern that fits most Govafy.com members: you have a better way to do something the agency cares about, and you want them to let you do it.
>
> The sample below is a fictional proposal from a fictional company to a realistic federal audience. The structure, regulation citations, and FAR 15.609 markings are real and compliant. The company, people, and numbers are invented.
>
> A "HOW TO ADAPT THIS TEMPLATE" section appears at the end.

## PART 2 — TECHNICAL INFORMATION (FAR 15.605(b))

**2.1 Title and Abstract**

**Title:** Veteran-to-Civilian Talent Pipeline Platform for Federal Hiring

**Abstract (198 words):**

Meridian Workforce Solutions, LLC proposes a 24-month services effort to deploy its proprietary PathMatch Engine talent-matching platform in support of the Department of Veterans Affairs' civilian workforce hiring mission. The VA is the second-largest federal employer and has publicly identified time-to-hire and veteran-candidate conversion as two of its most persistent workforce challenges. Meridian's PathMatch Engine is a validated matching platform that translates military occupational specialty (MOS) codes into VA civilian position series, automatically identifies skill gaps, and routes candidates through a streamlined assessment pathway. In a 14-month pilot with two state workforce agencies, PathMatch reduced average time-to-hire by 31% and increased veteran retention at 12 months by 22%. This proposal offers to deploy PathMatch at three VA regional hiring centers as an integrated service, measure outcomes against baseline, and deliver a final report with recommendations for enterprise-wide adoption. Total estimated price: $2,450,000 over 24 months. Meridian is an SBA-certified 8(a) and SDVOSB firm, providing the VA with a direct sole-source pathway under FAR Subpart 19.8 for award amounts below the 8(a) sole-source ceiling. This effort directly supports the VA Human Capital Strategic Plan hiring modernization objectives.

**2.2 Objectives**

Meridian proposes the following specific, measurable objectives:

1. **Deploy the PathMatch Engine** at three VA regional hiring centers (to be selected jointly with VA HR&A leadership) within six months of contract award.

2. **Reduce average time-to-hire** for veteran candidates at the pilot centers by at least 25% compared to a pre-deployment baseline, measured from job posting to tentative job offer.

3. **Increase veteran candidate conversion** (the rate at which qualified veteran applicants accept VA civilian positions) at the pilot centers by at least 15% compared to the pre-deployment baseline.

4. **Improve 12-month retention** of veteran hires at the pilot centers by at least 10 percentage points compared to the pre-deployment baseline.

5. **Deliver a formal recommendations report** to VA HR&A leadership at month 22, including outcomes data, deployment playbook, cost model for enterprise-wide rollout, and lessons learned.

**2.3 Method of Approach**

The proposed effort will execute in four phases over 24 months.

**Phase 1 — Baseline and Setup (Months 1–6).** Meridian will work with VA HR&A leadership to select the three pilot regional hiring centers and collect 24 months of baseline hiring data (time-to-hire, veteran applicant volume, veteran conversion rate, 12-month retention). In parallel, Meridian will configure the PathMatch Engine for the VA position series catalog and integrate with the VA's existing USA Staffing workflow. Deliverables include a Baseline Report, a Configuration Document, and a Deployment Readiness Assessment.

**Phase 2 — Guided Pilot Deployment (Months 7–15).** Meridian will deploy PathMatch at the three selected hiring centers and embed one Meridian workforce specialist at each site to support hiring managers and HR specialists during the initial adoption period. Meridian staff will track every hiring action through the platform and collect structured feedback from users. Deliverables include Monthly Deployment Reports, User Feedback Summaries, and a Mid-Pilot Data Review at month 12.

**Phase 3 — Steady-State Operations and Measurement (Months 16–22).** Meridian will transition from embedded support to help-desk and analytics support as the pilot sites operate PathMatch independently. During this phase Meridian will collect outcomes data, compare to the pre-deployment baseline, and analyze the retention cohort from Phase 2 hires. Deliverables include Quarterly Outcomes Reports.

**Phase 4 — Recommendations and Transition (Months 23–24).** Meridian will consolidate all findings into a final Recommendations Report, conduct briefings with VA HR&A leadership, and provide a transition package to the VA for continued operation (or enterprise expansion). Deliverables include the Final Recommendations Report, a Transition Playbook, and an Enterprise Rollout Cost Model.

**2.4 Anticipated Results**

At the conclusion of this 24-month effort, the VA will have:

- **Three VA regional hiring centers** operating with the PathMatch Engine, with measured reductions in time-to-hire, improvements in veteran conversion, and improvements in 12-month retention relative to baseline.
- **A validated outcomes data set** showing whether the platform meets the stated performance objectives, suitable for supporting an enterprise-wide adoption decision.
- **A complete deployment playbook** documenting every step needed to replicate the deployment at additional VA sites.
- **A cost model** for enterprise-wide rollout including license costs, integration costs, change management costs, and estimated return on investment.
- **A trained cadre** of VA HR staff at three centers with experience operating and benefiting from the platform.

**Based on pilot data** (available in the proprietary sheets of this proposal), Meridian's preliminary estimate is that enterprise-wide adoption of PathMatch across VA civilian hiring could result in annual savings of 180,000–220,000 staff hours in HR specialist time, plus a material improvement in mission readiness stemming from faster hiring and better retention.

**2.5 Mission Support Statement**

This effort directly supports the Department of Veterans Affairs' Human Capital Strategic Plan and the Office of Human Resources and Administration's mission to *"recruit, develop, and retain a high-performing workforce that is deeply committed to the VA's mission of serving veterans and their families."*

Specifically, the proposed work addresses three publicly identified VA human capital priorities:

1. **Hiring modernization.** VA has publicly committed to modernizing its hiring processes to reduce time-to-hire and compete effectively with private-sector employers for talent. The PathMatch Engine directly addresses time-to-hire as a measurable outcome.

2. **Veteran hiring as a VA priority.** The VA's mission is to serve veterans, and veterans themselves are a natural talent pool for VA civilian roles. VA has consistently identified veteran hiring as a strategic priority and has dedicated programs to support it. PathMatch amplifies those programs by automating the MOS-to-civilian-series matching process that has historically required manual translation by HR specialists.

3. **Retention and workforce stability.** The VA Inspector General and GAO have both identified workforce retention as a material risk to VA mission delivery, particularly in the health administration and benefits administration functions. PathMatch's pilot data shows measurable retention improvement for veteran hires, addressing this risk directly.

Meridian's 8(a) certification and SDVOSB status provide the VA with a direct sole-source contracting pathway under FAR 19.8, which is particularly appropriate for a workforce initiative focused on veteran outcomes.

## HOW TO ADAPT THIS TEMPLATE

**Things you must change:**

- [ ] Company name, address, contact information throughout
- [ ] Key personnel names and biographies in Section 2.6
- [ ] Technical content of the title, abstract, objectives, approach, and results (Sections 2.1–2.4)
- [ ] Target agency and mission support statement (Section 2.5) — cite YOUR target agency's actual strategic priorities
- [ ] Government support needed (Section 2.7)
- [ ] Cost estimates (Section 3.1) — use your real numbers
- [ ] Past performance (Section 3.5) — use your actual contracts and references
- [ ] Certifications on cover page and in Sections 1.1 and 2.5
- [ ] Proprietary data descriptions in Section 1.3
- [ ] Prior agency contacts (Section 3.7)

**Things you must keep unchanged:**

- [ ] The exact text of the FAR 15.609(a) title-page legend
- [ ] The three-part structure (Basic / Technical / Supporting)
- [ ] The FAR section citations in the headings
- [ ] The disclosure of other parties receiving the proposal (Section 1.4)
- [ ] The prior agency contacts disclosure (Section 3.7)

**Key adaptation tips specific to this template:**

1. **The mission support statement is the most important section.** Do not copy this template's mission language — rewrite it with quotes from YOUR target agency's strategic plan, published priorities, and IG or GAO reports. The more specifically you cite the agency's own language, the more convincing the proposal.

2. **The "innovative approach" has to be genuine.** A Mission-Solution proposal is high-risk because the agency likely already has a way to do what you're proposing. You have to articulate clearly why your approach is *materially* different and better — not just "we do it better," but *why* (a proprietary tool, a patented process, unique data, exclusive partnerships).

3. **Quantify everything.** Services proposals that lack numbers get rejected for cost realism failures. Hours, dollars, percentages, baseline-to-target changes — put real numbers in every section where you can.

4. **Set-aside certifications are a huge advantage.** If you hold 8(a), HUBZone, WOSB, or SDVOSB status, highlight it. These give the contracting officer a legal sole-source pathway that is much easier to justify than a full FAR 6.302-1 J&A.
