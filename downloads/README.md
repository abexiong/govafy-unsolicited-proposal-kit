# Downloadable Word Documents

This folder contains the **Word (`.docx`) versions** of the Govafy.com Unsolicited Proposal Kit — the master guide and all nine sample proposals — for users who prefer editing in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, or Apple Pages rather than in Markdown.

## How to download a single file

1. Click the file you want below (or navigate to it in the list at the top of this folder).
2. On the next screen, click the **Download raw file** button — it's the small icon in the top right of the file viewer (it looks like a downward arrow).
3. The file will save to your computer's Downloads folder.

## How to download everything at once

Go to the [main repository page](../) and click the green **Code** button → **Download ZIP**. That gives you the entire kit including this folder.

## What's in here

### The master guide

- `Govafy Guide to Writing Unsolicited Proposals.docx` — the full playbook, 12 sections and 9 appendices.

### General sample proposals

- `General Sample 1 - Innovation and R&D.docx` — for novel technology / patent / research pitches.
- `General Sample 2 - Mission-Solution.docx` — services or operational solutions to known mission needs. **Most broadly applicable template.**
- `General Sample 3 - Crisis and Rapid-Response.docx` — turnkey response to an urgent or emerging need.

### Agency-specific sample proposals

- `Specific Sample 1 - SentinelMind (DCSA).docx` — insider-threat analytics pitched to the Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency.
- `Specific Sample 2 - NeuroEdge (USSOCOM).docx` — human performance technology pitched to U.S. Special Operations Command.
- `Specific Sample 3 - ForgeForward (MCSC).docx` — next-generation additive manufacturing pitched to Marine Corps Systems Command. **Most important teaching example in the kit.**
- `Specific Sample 4 - FedFacility IQ (GSA PBS).docx` — facilities intelligence pitched to GSA Public Buildings Service.
- `Specific Sample 5 - LeadFed (OPM).docx` — executive leadership development pitched to the Office of Personnel Management.
- `Specific Sample 6 - ReadyRelief (FEMA).docx` — disaster logistics pitched to the Federal Emergency Management Agency.

## A reminder about the samples

**All nine samples are explicitly fictional.** The companies, personnel, patent numbers, pilot data, prices, and email addresses are invented. The agencies, regulations, program names, and mission language are real and compliant. **Never let a fictional detail from a sample leak into a real submission.**

## Source of truth

These `.docx` files are generated automatically from the Markdown source files in [`references/`](../references). If you spot a difference between a `.docx` and its `.md` counterpart, the `.md` is authoritative — and please [open an issue](../../../issues) so we can regenerate the Word file.
